Tribute to Mother Teresa

Introduction

Mother Teresa, also known as Saint Teresa of Calcutta, was a beacon of compassion and love in the modern world. Born on August 26, 1910, in Skopje, Macedonia, she dedicated her life to serving the poorest of the poor. Her unwavering commitment to helping those in need made her a global symbol of selflessness and humanitarianism.

Early Life

Mother Teresa, an iconic figure of compassion and selflessness, made remarkable contributions to society during her lifetime. She founded the Missionaries of Charity, a global organization that established hospitals, clinics, orphanages, and homes for the dying, offering medical care, shelter, and dignity to the impoverished. Mother Teresa's dedication to leprosy rehabilitation and her advocacy for peace earned her the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979, furthering her mission to alleviate human suffering. Her enduring legacy inspires countless individuals to follow her example, proving that small acts of great love can indeed change the world.
